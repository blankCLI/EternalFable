MIRROR-VENDING PROTOCOL

The operator found the vending machine behind the service corridor after the
third stairwell repeated. It sold no food. Every slot held a small mirror in a
paper sleeve, each sleeve printed with the same receipt number.

When the operator inserted a coin, the monitor above the machine woke and
displayed:

  PLEASE CHOOSE THE VERSION THAT LEFT FIRST.

Button A played static.
Button B played a child voice counting backwards from a number the operator did
not remember learning.
Button C opened the return tray and delivered a warm key wrapped in black wire.

The key did not fit any door in the corridor. It fit the reflection of a door in
the mirror from slot B7. The operator turned it there. Somewhere behind the
glass, a clerk said thank you in the operator's voice.

The corridor lights went out one at a time. The vending machine printed a final
receipt:

  CHANGE DUE: ONE MEMORY
  DISPENSED: ONE EXIT
  REMAINING BALANCE: YOUR SHADOW

The stairwell appeared again, now descending upward.
